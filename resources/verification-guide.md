# Verification Guide

## Why Verification Matters

AI systems sometimes provide:

* Incorrect facts
* Outdated information
* Invented sources
* Overconfident conclusions

Verification is one of the most valuable skills you can develop.

---

# The Verification Process

Step 1

Generate information using AI.

Step 2

Identify important claims.

Step 3

Check independent sources.

Step 4

Evaluate source quality.

Step 5

Adjust confidence level.

---

# Source Hierarchy

Generally speaking:

Highest Confidence

* Official organizations
* Government sources
* Academic research

Moderate Confidence

* Major publications
* Industry reports

Lower Confidence

* Blogs
* Social media
* Anonymous posts

---

# Confidence Levels

## High Confidence

Multiple reliable sources agree.

## Medium Confidence

Some evidence exists but uncertainty remains.

## Low Confidence

Evidence is weak or conflicting.

---

# Questions to Ask

Who created this information?

Why was it created?

When was it created?

Can it be verified elsewhere?

What assumptions are being made?

---

# Common Verification Mistakes

* Trusting the first answer
* Using only one source
* Ignoring conflicting evidence
* Assuming AI is always correct

---

# Verification Checklist

□ Multiple sources reviewed

□ Sources are credible

□ Information is current

□ Claims are supported

□ Confidence level assigned
